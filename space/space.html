Our Inspiration
The “overview effect” is described by astronauts as an irresistible realization upon seeing Earth from space, of the fragility and singularity of our world. National boundaries vanish, the conflicts that divide people become less important, and the need to create a planetary society with the united will to protect this "pale blue dot" becomes both obvious and imperative.

Our Mission

To promote social awareness and global citizenship — to compel everyone to care about human struggles beyond the comfort of our daily routines.

Our Principles

  • 1

    Equality

    Our map breaks the limits of “front page real estate” and presents struggles from around the world as event blips on a single map. Each blip is the same size and conveys the same importance, no matter their ideology, regardless of whether they are “trending”. We strive to capture every event which meets a preset threshold for its category.

  • 2

    Objectiveness

    No speculation or talking heads, no re-framing of issues, or use of loaded words such as “terrorism”. Cartogracy presents users with hard data: lives lost, territories affected, related conflicts. We encourage readers to explore our wealth of curated content and draw their own conclusions.

  • 3

    Intuitiveness

    Cartogracy strives to convey as much information as intuitively and concisely as possible. The foundation of our site is the conflict map, which provide context for otherwise complicated socio-political issues, and serves as navigation for an in depth look at each conflict.
